## 6. Legal / Entity / Liability Infrastructure (WAC Competitors)

**No YC company in any scanned batch is building legal entity infrastructure for AI agents.**

The closest are:
- [**truthsystems**](https://www.truthsystems.ai) — governance for legal/professional firms, but for compliance monitoring, not entity creation
- [**Platus**](https://www.platus.com) — "Instant Legal Infrastructure" but it's a legal AI workspace, not agent entity formation
- [**Tower**](https://withtower.com) — legal due diligence AI, not entity infrastructure
- [**Salus**](https://www.usesalus.ai/) — action guardrails could serve as a compliance layer for agent entities

**This confirms the Agent LLC Act occupies a white space.** No one in YC is working on:
- Legal entity formation for agents
- Agent liability frameworks
- Agent corporate governance
- Fiduciary structures for autonomous agents
- Agent dispute resolution mechanisms

## 7. Top 10 Most Strategically Relevant Companies for WAC

Ranked by direct relevance to the Agent LLC Act and agent-as-economic-actor thesis:

### 1. [**Locus**](https://paywithlocus.com) (Fall 2025) — Payment infrastructure for agents
WHY: Solves the exact "how does an agent hold and spend money" problem. USDC escrow + spending policies = financial controls for Agent LLCs. Natural integration partner.

### 2. [**Alter**](https://alterai.dev) (Summer 2025) — Zero-trust identity for agents
WHY: Agent LLCs need identity. Alter provides parameter-level auth, ephemeral credentials, and full audit trails — the identity layer Agent LLC Act would require.

### 4. [**Multifactor**](https://multifactor.com) (Fall 2025) — Zero-trust credential sharing for agents
WHY: Agents acting as economic entities need to access online services without exposing credentials. Multifactor solves this with temporary session tokens and granular permissions.

## 8. Key Observations

### The agent infrastructure stack is rapidly forming
In just 5 batches, YC has funded companies across every layer: payments, identity, execution, memory, observability, safety, tools, and even marketing. This is an emerging platform stack, not isolated experiments.

### The missing layer is legal/entity infrastructure
Nobody is building the legal personality layer. Every other infrastructure need (run, pay, authenticate, remember, monitor) has multiple YC companies competing. Legal entity creation, liability frameworks, and dispute resolution remain a complete white space.

### Payments + Identity are the hottest layers
4 companies in payments, 4 in identity — and these have the highest keyword relevance scores. The market clearly recognizes agents need to pay and authenticate.

### The AGENT-AS-PRODUCT list is the customer list for Agent LLC
57 companies building agents that DO jobs. Each of these is a potential customer for Agent LLC infrastructure. Agents handling insurance claims, mortgage servicing, compliance, customer service, and financial operations are exactly the use cases that would benefit from legal entity status.

### Batch trend: agent infra is accelerating
- W24/S24/F24: Mostly foundational (observability, RAG, testing)
- W25: Identity + execution emerging ([A1Base](https://www.a1base.com/), [Butter](https://butter.dev), [Truffle AI](https://www.trytruffle.ai/))
- X25/Spring 2025: MCP explosion ([Klavis](https://www.klavis.ai/), [Golf](https://golf.dev/), [Airweave](https://airweave.ai), [Capacitive](https://capacitive.ai/))
- Summer 2025: Full stack ([Alter](https://alterai.dev), [Kernel](https://www.kernel.sh), [AgentMail](https://agentmail.to), [Cyberdesk](https://www.cyberdesk.io), [truthsystems](https://www.truthsystems.ai))
- Fall 2025: Economic agents arriving ([Locus](https://paywithlocus.com), [Multifactor](https://multifactor.com), [Agentic Fabriq](https://www.agenticfabriq.com), [Bear](https://usebear.ai))
- Winter 2026: Payments + governance ([Orthogonal](https://orthogonal.com), [Maven](https://www.trymaven.com), [Salus](https://www.usesalus.ai/), [Sentrial](https://sentrial.com/))
